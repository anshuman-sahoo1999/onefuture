biography: |
  Shri Guru Prasad Mishra, who joined the Odisha Administrative Service in 1979 and was promoted to the Indian Administrative Service in 1991 for his exemplary work, is widely respected for his integrity, empathy, and people-centric governance. His early postings in tribal regions, including roles in Sundargarh as Project Administrator and ADM (Welfare), saw him champion inclusive development and pioneer participatory forest protection through the creation of Bana Suraksha Samitis.

  As Officer on Special Duty to the Chief Secretary, Odisha, he coordinated files across 32 departments, gaining comprehensive insight into statewide governance and policy frameworks. Later, as Collector of Jharsuguda, he provided visionary leadership during a period of rapid industrial growth, balancing economic expansion with welfare and infrastructure development.

  Across decades of service, Shri Mishra has been recognized for his strategic clarity, community empowerment initiatives, and unwavering dedication to the holistic development of Odisha.
